Allegory of the cave

A parable by Plato that is presented as an allegory with many meanings and interpretations. Most notably, the parable is an analogue to a philosopher's life. In an another interpretation the parable can be viewed as an argument suggesting that what we percieve as reality with our senses might very well be just an illusion. It could be just that the objects we percieve to be real maybe a reflection/projection of some other 'form' which is more real, just like a shadow of an object is not the real object itself!
